Laab Gai - Lao Minced Chicken Salad

Laab Gai is a minced chicken salad flavored with lime, fish sauce, herbs, and vegatables.

This dish originated from Laos and Northeastern Thailand. It's loaded with fresh herbs, spices, and vegatables. Laab Gai is served hot or room temperature with green leaf lettuce and a side of sticky rice or jasmine rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon peanut oil
  • 4 cloves garlic minced
  • 1/2 inch piece ginger minced
  • 1 pound ground chicken
  • 1-2 teaspoons cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 cup water optional
  • 1 tablespoon toasted rice powder
  • 1 medium red onion thinly sliced
  • 1 red pepper chopped
  • 1/4 cup cilantro chopped
  • 1/4 cup mint chopped
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 limes juiced
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Garnish: Lime wedges
  • Garnish: Green leaf lettuce

Directions

  1. In a wok, drizzle peanut oil and heat to medium heat. Add garlic and ginger. Fry until fragrant, 30 seconds.
  2. Stir in the chicken, breaking it up into small pieces, and the cayenne pepper. If the chicken gets too dry, add up to 1/2 cup water. Fry, stirring often, until cooked through.
  3. Remove from heat and add the rice powder, red onion, red pepper, cilantro, and mint.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the fish sauce, lime juice, and sugar. Toss with the chicken. Serve with the lettuce leaves and rice.
